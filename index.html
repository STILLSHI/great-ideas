The Premise

A fact is an answer. A way of thinking is a method for generating answers.

Civilizations lose their facts and rediscover them. What actually compounds across the centuries is something subtler: the set of cognitive moves humans invented to make sense of things — to doubt, to abstract, to invert, to model, to update, to subtract.

This atlas gathers the durable ones, from Bronze-Age cosmology to information theory, and sorts them two ways at once: by the lens they hand you, and by the age that produced them. A handful of meta-patterns — the Modes — recur beneath them all.

Ideas here aren't kept behind glass. Each card traces its lineage — what it drew on, what it fed — and a Proving Ground follows the great moves out of the library and into moonshots, constitutions, and eradications.

Then the atlas does the thing every thinker here did: it recombines. The final section forges new ideas by crossing old ones — because that, too, is a way of thinking.
